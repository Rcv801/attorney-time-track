# Top 5 Competitors for Attorney Time Track
**Date:** 2026-02-15  
**Positioning:** Lean billing app for solo/small firm attorneys ($19-29/user/mo)  
**Our features:** Time tracking, invoicing, trust/IOLTA accounting, LEDES export

---

## Executive Summary

Attorney Time Track's closest competitors are **not** the full practice management suites (Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther) ‚Äî those are bloated and expensive. Our real competition is the handful of lean, billing-focused tools that a disgruntled Clio customer would actually consider. After extensive research, here are the 5 tools we're directly fighting for the same customer.

**TL;DR of our competitive position:**
- Time59 beats us on price but loses on UX and mobile
- Bill4Time is the most direct threat but is pricing themselves out of our market
- TimeSolv has great features but costs too much for solos
- LeanLaw requires QuickBooks (we don't) and costs more
- CaseFox has a free tier but lacks polish and trust accounting depth

**Our key differentiators at $19-29/mo:**
1. Trust/IOLTA accounting included at base tier (competitors charge $45-79 for this)
2. LEDES export at base tier (competitors lock behind $55-89 plans)
3. No QuickBooks dependency
4. Modern mobile experience
5. Price point that doesn't exist in the market right now

### Their Weakness We Exploit
**Price + mobile + feature gating.** A solo attorney who needs trust accounting is looking at $59/mo with Bill4Time and getting a buggy mobile app. We offer the same core features at $19-29 with a modern mobile experience.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Lead with price + trust accounting.** No one else offers trust/IOLTA + LEDES at $19-29. This is our hook.

2. **Mobile-first marketing.** Bill4Time's biggest weakness is their mobile app. Every Reddit complaint is ammunition for us.

3. **Target the "spreadsheet attorneys."** Reddit shows many solos use Excel/OneNote because they can't justify $49+/mo. We're the product that makes them stop.

4. **Don't compete with Time59 on price.** They're $199/year. We can't match that. Instead, compete on UX, mobile, and integrations. Frame it as "Time59 is cheap but old. We're affordable and modern."

5. **Simple pricing page.** TimeSolv and LeanLaw have confusing pricing. Bill4Time has hidden feature gating. Our pricing should be dead simple: one plan, everything included, $X/mo.

6. **Data portability as a selling point.** Reddit users are burned by vendor lock-in (Lawcus, Clio). Emphasize easy data export and no contracts.
